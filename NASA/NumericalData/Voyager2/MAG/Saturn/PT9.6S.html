Voyager 2 Saturn Magnetic Field in Kronographic L1 Coordinates, Resampled, Version 1.1, 9.6 s Data

ResourceID
spase://NASA/NumericalData/Voyager2/MAG/Saturn/PT9.6S

Description
  • Data Set Overview
  • =================

This Data Set includes Voyager 2 Saturn Encounter Magnetometer Data from the Low Field Magnetometer (LFM) resampled at a 9.6 s Sample Rate from the 60 ms Instrument Sampling Rate. Data Coverage begins in the Solar Wind and continues until at least the first Magnetopause Crossing. The Data are given in Kronographic (L1) Coordinates.

    1. Time - Universal Time of the Sample in the Format YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SS.SSSZ
    1. Spacecraft Clock Values - m65536:mod60:fds_line
    1. mag_id - Magnetometer ID: 1=LFM, 2=HFM
    1. Br - Radial Magnetic Field Component, Saturn to Spacecraft
    1. Btheta - North/South Magnetic Field Component, positive southward, L1 Coordinates
    1. Bphi - Azimuthal Magnetic Field Component, positive eastward, L1 Coordinates
    1. Bmag - Magnitude of the averaged Magnetic Field Components
    1. avg_Bmag - Average of the Magnetic Field Magnitudes
    1. delta - Magnetic Latitude, delta=asin(Btheta/Bmag)
    1. lambda - Magnetic Longitude, lambda=180.0-atan(Bphi/-Br)
    1. rms_br - Br RMS Value
    1. rms_bt - Bt RMS Value
    1. rms_bn - Bn RMS Value
    1. npts - Number of Points in the Average

All Magnetic Field Observations are measured in nT. All of the Magnetic Field Data are calibrated (see the Instrument Calibration Description for more Details). These Data are provided in a Saturn centered Spherical System (L1), based on the Saturn Longitude System (Voyager Measurements of the Rotation Period of Saturn's Magnetic Field, Desch and Kaiser, JGR, 8, 253, 1981). The Magnetic Field Components are:

  • Br - Radial Magnetic Field Component, along the Saturn to Spacecraft Line, positive away from Saturn
  • Btheta - North/South (L1) Magnetic Field Component, positive southward
  • Bphi - Azimuthal (L1) Magnetic Field Component, positive eastward
